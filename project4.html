About

Problem Statement: People with PTSD need to find a way to heal the broken through an accessible retreat that leads them to listen to their inner wisdom.

Which design solutions can help us with this challenge?

Empathizing with our User persona, we decided that people with PTSD need guidance. Through visual stimulation, we created a journey where the User is capable to go through 7 days of retreat in an app. Each day contains different challenges to introduce them to better habits such as breathing, meditating, journaling, body scan, self-reflection, and many more. The journey will be separated into 3 different Cosmos, each one representing an element. Water, Wind, and Warmth will help them to engage in the exercises and relate to some characteristics that will make the journey more enjoyable.

Style Guide

Colors and backgrounds are used to have a visual stimulation that can assemble to inner and outer worlds. Working on micro-interactions and animations we created usability that recognizes the visibility of system status, an aesthetically minimalist design, and consistency.

Prototype

We start the app with an animated welcome message, inviting the user to take 3 deep breaths in.

After the breathing exercise, we created a Guided Animation to introduce the user to the upcoming journey and how to find himself on the screens. It is possible also to choose between starting the journey alone or creating a circle with other users.

The centered Cosmo becomes a visual form that serves as a guide through the App and also contains messages. After the guided animation is possible to start on your 1st day of the journey. Staring on the Water Cosmo.

Each Element has a different color. On your first day on one of these elements, there will be an intro video about the benefits of meeting this element and what the user is about to learn and experience.

Other screens such as dashboard and journaling are available in the app.

At the end of the journey, it is possible to share the app with friends, customize your journey by creating your own routines, habits, and exercises, or start again.
